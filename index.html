About Me

A curious creative finding the parallels between music
and data science.

My programming journey began as a child by making an utter mess of the files on the family computer. Years later in high school I would write programs for my TI-83 calculator to aid me in solving problems in math and physics. Looking back I recognize that I have always possessed a curiosity about this aspect of technology.

This same curiosity would follow me into adulthood in service of my 12-plus year career as a musician and composer. While performing on stages throughout the world and connecting with many different people of different languages and cultures is an absolutely wonderful pastime; I also love the art and science of dedicating time carefully creating something from scratch. As a composer I honed my skills at pairing melody, harmony, and rhythm and putting notes and symbols on paper to be interpreted by other musicians in order to create something effective, purposeful, and unique.

When I decided to transition into a data science career I realized that my experiences as a musician and composer have already aided me tremendously in the journey. There are an abundance of skills that data scientists are called upon to use in their development of various projects that are equally as prevalent in composing. Indeed, building a well structured and written kernel is an act of composition.

We both bring an analytical and creative mindset to the project to accomplish the task at hand. Where a composer meticulously searches for the right sound to convey his or her intention often referencing the work of others, so does a data scientist try and retry different codes until executed correctly. Where a musician and composer must develop good interpersonal and communication skills in order to work with other musicians, so does the data scientist need to be very intentional about documenting his or her workflow so that other team members can accomplish the task at hand with minimal interference.

I am excited about the prospect of discovering even more parallels and applying so many of the skills I have developed as a musician and composer to my work as a data scientist.
